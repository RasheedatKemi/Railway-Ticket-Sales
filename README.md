# Railway-Ticket-Sales
End-to-end analysis of a UK railway ticket sales dataset — data cleaning, descriptive analysis, journey performance, and refund insights — delivered with an interactive dashboard built in Excel/Power BI.

## Overview
This project analyzes a railway ticket sales dataset covering ticket purchases,
journeys, delays, pricing, payment methods, and customer attributes. The goal is
to clean the raw data, perform structured analysis, and surface clear, actionable
business insights through a well-designed dashboard.

Built entirely using  Microsoft Power BI, this project demonstrates the
full analytics workflow — from raw data to executive-ready recommendations.

## 🎯 Objectives
- Clean and validate the dataset (missing values, duplicates, date/time formatting)
- Generate core descriptive metrics (revenue, pricing, top stations, payment trends)
- Evaluate journey performance ( delay causes, peak times)
- Assess the revenue impact of refunds
- Translate findings into business insights and recommendations

## 🔍 Key Analysis Areas
| Section | Focus |

| **Descriptive Analysis** | Total revenue, avg. ticket price, % of delayed journeys & total refund requests |

## 💡 Key Takeaways
* Journey Delays Impact Customer Experience: Approximately 13% of all journeys were delayed, with delayed services leading to a significant increase in refund requests and journey cancellations.
  
* Primary Causes of Delays: The most common operational issues affecting service reliability were signal failures, staff shortages, and adverse weather conditions.

* Strong Revenue Performance: The railway network generated a total revenue of 742K, demonstrating strong passenger demand despite operational disruptions.
  
* Peak Revenue Periods: Morning journeys were the highest revenue contributors (297K), followed by evening journeys (169K), indicating that commuter travel drives the majority of revenue.
  
* Customer Payment Preference: Credit cards were the most frequently used payment method, highlighting the importance of maintaining efficient digital payment systems.
  
* Busiest Railway Stations: Manchester Piccadilly, London Euston, Birmingham New Street, and Liverpool Lime Street recorded the highest passenger traffic, making them critical hubs for capacity planning and operational improvements.
  
* Business Insight: Reducing delays caused by signal failures, staffing constraints, and weather-related disruptions—particularly during high-demand morning and evening periods—could improve customer satisfaction, reduce refund costs, and protect revenue.
